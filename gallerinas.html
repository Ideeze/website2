    Gallerinas

    Gallerinas is an art gallery tour booking platform for art lovers, students, and busy professionals who would like to plan out time to enjoying social activities.

    Role

    UX research & design - designing an art gallery tour booking app from conception to delivery

    Duration

    February 2022 - July 2022

    Team

    Uche Orjinta, Jennifer Orjinta

    The problem

    Busy professionals and students cannot find the right art-galleries to visit in real-time during their free time.

    The goal

    Design an app that will let users easily search & book art gallery tours online.

    Starting point

    Understanding the user

    Alfie is a mid-career professional at a large accounting firm who was recently promoted, He enjoys the nightlife & social scene in his city and would love to have more time for his personal life. He works hard & is a team player, and wants his team to be ready for another promotion within a year or two.

    1) Persona info

    Market Survey

    Competitive Audit

    I audited 4 competitors; 'direct & indirect' to compare the tour booking experience of each competitor's website as a first-time visitor.

    2) Audit of primary & secondar competitors

    Ideation

    Paper wireframes

    My Research partner 'Jenny' & I did a quick ideation exercise to come up with ideas for how to address gaps we identified in the competitive audit. our focus was on a quick & convenient art gallery searing, eveluation & booking process.

    3) Crazy 8 to paper wireframes

    Starting the design

    Digital wireframes

    Using the completed set of digital wireframes, I created a low-fidelity prototype. The primary user flow I connected was searching for and booking an art gallery tour, so the prototype could be used in a usability study

    4) lofi-prototyping

    Site mapping

    Desktop Site Mapping

    My goal was to make the navigation easy and time saving, and to ensure that I created a site map; to make sure the user could easily navigate through the pages from more than one location.

    5) Website design challenge

    Design Challenges

    Desktop usability study feedback

    Early designs allowed for easy searching of activities, but after the usability studies, I simplified the search options to limit the use of a search bar on the homescreen by creation custom search features.

    6) Desktop design challenge

    Going forward

    responsive designs

    Users of the Amigos app would use both their mobile devices while outdoors and their desktop devices while at home, and this made me create responsive designs for both mobile and desktop to accomodation both user needs.

    7) Responsive desktop designs

    Prototyping

    High fidelity prototype

    The high-fidelity prototype followed the same user flow as the low-fidelity prototype, including changes made after the usability study.

    8) Desktop version

    Conclusion

    Take aways

    Overall we succeded in designing an app that makes it fun and easy for users to think about planning, searching and booking art gallery tours using our site. Our next steps involve carrying out another usability study to refine the designs & influence how Gallerinas app looks & flows.
