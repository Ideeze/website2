    Amigos

    Amigos is a health & safety lifestyle app for sporty individuals & single professionals to keep track of their health & safety routines when home alone or participating in outdoor activities.

    Role

    UX research & design - designing a health & safety app from conception to delivery

    Duration

    August 2022

    Team

    Uche Orjinta, Jennifer Orjinta

    The problem

    Sports enthusiasts & professionals who live alone do not have a way to keep track & manage their health & safety status in real-time.

    The goal

    Design an app that will let users easily track, manage, & share their health & safety status in real-time.

    Starting point

    Understanding the user

    I conducted research to understand the users I’m designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified was sports enthusiasts like Jonathan; a graduate living in Europe with his girlfriend, he is passionate about photography and car racing. and wants to enjoy more outdoor activities while also keeping track of his health status, so his girlfriend would not worry so much. .

    1) Persona info

    Market Survey

    Competitive Audit

    An audit of a few competitors’ products provided direction on gaps and opportunities to address with the Amigos app.

    2) Audit of primary & secondar competitors

    Ideation

    Paper wireframes

    I did a quick ideation exercise to come up with ideas for how to address gaps identified in the competitive audit. My focus was on simple location sharing & safety checks.

    3) From crazy 8 to paper wireframes

    Starting the design

    Site Mapping

    In oder to design a responsive site for both mobile & web users, I created the Amigos sitemap to guide the organizational structure of each screen design to ensure a consistent experience across devices.

    4) Website sitemapping

    Design Challenge 1

    Desktop usability study feedback

    Based on the insights from the usability studies , I applied design changes like animating product guides to make it easy for the users to understand how to use the product.

    5) Website design challenge

    Design Challenge 2

    Mobile usability study feedback

    Additional design changes included including a calendar to allow users book physical activities in advance.

    6) Mobile design challenge

    Going forward

    responsive designs

    Even though users would focus more on using the mobile version of the app, i created designs for both Desktop and mobile designs; to ensure that users could complete their activity from any device screen.

    7) Desktop & Mobile designs

    Prototyping

    High fidelity prototype

    The high-fidelity prototype followed the same user flow as the low-fidelity prototype, including changes made after the usability study.

    8) Mobile version

    Conclusion

    Take aways

    Ideating & designing Amigos confirmed my initial assumptions people relying on memory to keep track of health & safety routines, so it was extremely important to collaborate with my research partner;'Jennifer' to design a solution that was easy to use and could easily incorporate into the user's daily lifestyle.

    My next steps invole conducting another round of usability study for a marketplace feature to be added on the app; to develop a well-rounded experience for both outdoor and indoor users.
